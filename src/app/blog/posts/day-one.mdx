## Day One!

Today marks the beginning of my career as a professional photographer! I know the cover image has nothing to do with photography, but it nevertheless is a symbol of a new chapter of my life! 

Photography has been something I've enjoyed for a long time. Since my toddler years, I've been immersed in it.

***

I am very grateful that, in my junior year of high school, I rediscovered this passion when participating in SING! 2025 at Stuyvesant High School. Being a part of the Media & Memory crew is one of the best things that has ever happened to me.

After considering where I could make the most impact, I chose Media & Memory, knowing I’d enjoy documenting and capturing the showmaking process. The first few times I picked up my phone to record—cast rehearsing lines, tech building sets, dance crews choreographing—I didn’t think much of it. But over time, I noticed how little I had to think. The shots came naturally, my hands moving before my mind could catch up. When it came time to edit, Premiere Pro’s interface felt instantly familiar. Click here, drag there—I knew exactly where everything was. 

> **It felt like déjà vu. And then I remembered.**

Memories from a long time ago started surfacing—back when I was in Hong Kong, back when I was in Beijing. In each memory, I was holding one of my dad’s old cameras, taking pictures or videos of anything that caught my eye: animals, buildings, people, scenery, trees. One vivid night in 2012: picture a kid no more than a meter tall, weaving through the crowd at Tiananmen Square, carefree, with a camera the size of his head, capturing strangers. Another afternoon wandering in Hong Kong Park at age eight, snapping pictures of birds—one of which won me an instant camera after placing second in the 2016 NAE Global Campus photography competition.

Remembering those moments made me realize that I had never stopped loving photography and videography—I had just forgotten. Maybe it was COVID, the move from Hong Kong to NYC, or both—struggling to adjust to a new life and high school while dealing with long commutes and a hectic schedule. Somewhere along the way, it faded into the background. Rediscovering it through SING! felt like reconnecting with a part of myself I hadn’t realized I’d lost, and I promised myself I would never forget it again. I would make space for it in my life.

**And I did.**

Since SING, I’ve made photography and videography a regular part of my life, prioritizing it even when school and other commitments got busy. 

Every new project I start, every new tool I try, is another chance to chase the same blissful feeling I first discovered all those years ago as a toddler—only now, with intention. I’m excited to see where this passion will take me next—both in college, and beyond.
